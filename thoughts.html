Participatory Design in Educational Contexts: Methodologies and Outcomes

S. Llàcer Caro, J. Smith, M. García

This paper examines the use of participatory design methodologies in formal and informal educational settings. We explore how students, educators, and researchers can collaborate to create more inclusive and responsive learning environments, drawing on case studies across three institutions in southern Europe.

AI-Mediated Learning: Towards a Critical Framework for Design Research

S. Llàcer Caro, A. Kowalski

We present a critical framework for evaluating AI-mediated learning tools from a design research perspective. The framework integrates theories of situated learning, critical pedagogy, and human-computer interaction to assess the equity and effectiveness of AI systems in educational practice.

Co-design Methods for Community Engagement in Urban Contexts

S. Llàcer Caro, L. Nguyen, P. Martín

This article presents a comparative study of co-design methods deployed across three urban community projects. We analyse how different facilitation approaches affect participant engagement, ownership of outcomes, and the sustainability of the designed interventions beyond the research period.

Human-Centred Technology Design in K–12 Education

S. Llàcer Caro, R. Chen

We investigate how human-centred design principles can be applied to the development of technology for primary and secondary education. Through a design-based research approach conducted across six schools, we identify key tensions and opportunities in designing for young learners as both users and creators.

This chapter argues for the integration of systems thinking into design education curricula at all levels. Drawing on practice-based research and curriculum analysis, the author proposes a set of transferable pedagogical principles that bridge design, ecology, and social complexity.

Digital Fabrication and Making in Design Education

S. Llàcer Caro, D. Osei, F. Ramos

We examine the role of digital fabrication tools and maker culture in contemporary design education. Our mixed-methods study across three design schools explores how access to fabrication technologies influences creative practice, student agency, and the shifting boundaries between prototyping and production.
